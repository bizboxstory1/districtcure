About the Capitol Hill Neighborhood

Capitol Hill is one of Washington, DC's oldest and most iconic residential neighborhoods, stretching eastward behind the United States Capitol building. Known for its 19th-century rowhouses, tree-lined streets, and historic landmarks, the neighborhood is a bustling mix of federal workers, congressional staff, long-term residents, and young professionals. The area is anchored by the historic Eastern Market, a vibrant community hub where residents gather every weekend for fresh food, local art, and a bustling flea market.

Unlike some of DC's more transient areas, Capitol Hill has a deep-rooted sense of community. The neighborhood's residential streets offer a quiet, almost small-town feel despite being at the center of national politics. Historic commercial corridors like Barracks Row (8th Street SE) provide a diverse array of dining and retail options, making the neighborhood highly walkable and self-contained.

For cannabis consumers in Capitol Hill, convenience and discretion are key. Many residents balance demanding professional lives with a desire for high-quality wellness and relaxation products. This demographic often seeks out licensed, reliable dispensaries like District Cure that offer transparent lab testing, expert guidance, and discreet delivery options that fit seamlessly into a busy schedule.

While District Cure is located a few miles away on Georgia Avenue, our delivery service has become a trusted resource for Capitol Hill residents who prioritize safety, legality, and premium product selection over unregulated storefronts closer to home.

Legal Access & Medical Cannabis Registration

All cannabis purchases for Capitol Hill residents go through Washington DC's official medical cannabis program, regulated by the Alcoholic Beverage and Cannabis Administration (ABCA). District residents who are 21 and older can take advantage of the streamlined self-certification process, which allows them to register online in minutes without needing a doctor's recommendation. Upon completing the application, patients receive a temporary digital registration that can be used immediately at licensed retailers like District Cure. For the many out-of-state professionals and visitors in the Capitol Hill area, DC also offers reciprocity, accepting valid medical cannabis cards from other jurisdictions, ensuring legal and safe access for everyone.

Cannabis Use in Washington DC: By the Numbers

Washington, DC maintains one of the highest rates of cannabis use in the nation, with approximately one-third of adult residents reporting usage for medical or recreational purposes. As the city's legal framework has evolved, the registered medical cannabis patient population has surged past 116,000 active patients. This normalization of cannabis is clearly visible in neighborhoods like Capitol Hill, where use spans a wide demographic—from young staffers to established professionals and retirees—who view cannabis as a standard part of their wellness and relaxation routines.
