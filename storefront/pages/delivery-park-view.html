About the Park View Neighborhood

Park View is one of Washington's smaller, more residential rowhouse neighborhoods, wedged between Columbia Heights and Petworth along the Georgia Avenue corridor. The neighborhood takes its name from its proximity to what is now the Howard University campus and Malcolm X Park, and its streets are lined with modest Wardman-style rowhouses built in the early 20th century for federal government workers. It has long flown under the radar compared to its flashier neighbors, but that's part of its appeal — quiet, tight-knit, and increasingly popular with young families and Howard University staff.

Bruce Monroe Park serves as the neighborhood's community anchor, hosting the Saturday farmers market and neighborhood events, while the historic Park View Recreation Center gives residents green space without needing to travel far. Georgia Avenue itself has undergone a steady wave of small-business revitalization over the past decade, with new coffee shops, restaurants, and retail filling in what were once vacant storefronts — a shift that has made the corridor, and District Cure along with it, feel like a genuine neighborhood main street rather than just a through-route.

Park View's civic identity is closely tied to its neighborhood association, one of the more active in the District, which has pushed for streetscape improvements, traffic calming, and preservation of the area's modest but architecturally consistent rowhouse blocks. Because the neighborhood borders both Columbia Heights and the Howard University campus, it functions as a kind of connective tissue between two very different parts of the city — the dense commercial energy to the south and the historic academic institution to the east — while maintaining its own distinct, low-key residential character.

Housing turnover in Park View has been slower than in many nearby neighborhoods, meaning many households have lived on the same block for a decade or more. That stability shows up in how residents shop: less about discovering something new, more about maintaining a routine they've already refined over years of trial and error with different products and dosages.

Best-Selling Cannabis Products in This Area

Park View shoppers tend to favor value and consistency over novelty, and District Cure's flower program reflects that. Classic indica and hybrid strains in mid-weight jars are the most reliable sellers here, purchased by regulars who've settled into a routine rather than experimenting week to week. Because Park View has a strong contingent of longtime residents and Howard-affiliated staff, many customers are older and more experienced consumers who know exactly what cannabinoid and terpene profile works for them.

Edibles sell steadily in this neighborhood too, particularly slower-onset gummies and tinctures favored by residents managing chronic discomfort or sleep issues. Tinctures in particular see more traffic here than in some of the more nightlife-driven neighborhoods nearby, since Park View's customer base skews toward wellness-focused, lower-key use rather than social or recreational occasions. Pre-rolls remain a dependable category for residents who want a fast, no-fuss option after work, and CBD topicals are popular with the neighborhood's older demographic for joint and muscle relief.

District Cure's staff also point to a notable trend among Park View's older, longtime customers: a preference for products with clearly labeled cannabinoid ratios rather than high-THC potency alone. Balanced CBD:THC flower and low-dose edibles see steady repeat business from residents managing arthritis, sleep disruption, or general daily stress. Overall, Park View's buying pattern is less about trends and more about trust — residents come back to District Cure because the same knowledgeable staff, the same reliable inventory, and the same straightforward registration process are there every time.

Legal Access & Medical Cannabis Registration

Because Park View sits just outside DC's busiest commercial corridors, many residents here were among the earliest to register through the District's medical self-certification system when it simplified in 2022. DC residents 21 and older can complete the ABCA self-certification application online without a doctor's visit, and a temporary digital registration typically arrives within minutes, letting new patients shop at District Cure the same day. Renewals now run on a two-year cycle for anyone registered after March 2025. Because so many Park View residents registered years ago, District Cure's team frequently helps longtime patients navigate renewal notices and update their registration details rather than walking brand-new customers through the process from scratch.

Cannabis Use in Washington DC: By the Numbers

DC's Alcoholic Beverage and Cannabis Administration reported more than 116,000 active registered medical cannabis patients as of April 2026, a number that has grown steadily since self-certification removed the doctor's-visit requirement for most adults. Nationally, cannabis added an estimated $149 billion to the U.S. economy in 2025, and DC's per-capita usage rate — around a third of adults — remains among the highest of any state or district, driven in large part by neighborhoods like Park View where cannabis use has long been a normalized, everyday part of life rather than a novelty.
