About the Columbia Heights Neighborhood

Columbia Heights is one of the most densely populated and culturally rich neighborhoods in the District, anchored by the Columbia Heights Metro station on the Green and Yellow Lines and the DCUSA shopping complex that brought a Target, Best Buy, and dozens of restaurants to the corridor in the 2000s. The neighborhood's rowhouse blocks, many built in the early 1900s, house a mix of longtime Latino and Caribbean families, Howard University-adjacent students, and newer residents drawn by the walkability and nightlife along 14th Street.

Meridian Hill Park, known locally as Malcolm X Park, borders the neighborhood's southern edge and draws crowds for its weekend drum circles and Italian Renaissance-style terraces. The Tivoli Theatre, a restored 1924 movie palace, now anchors the GALA Hispanic Theatre and adds a cultural landmark feel to the area. With its mix of dense apartment buildings, historic rowhouses, and constant foot traffic, Columbia Heights has one of the highest population densities in DC, which is exactly why a licensed, walkable dispensary matters so much to the people who live here.

Columbia Heights' population also skews younger on average than many surrounding DC neighborhoods, with a large share of renters in their twenties and thirties who moved here for Metro access and nightlife proximity without wanting to pay Adams Morgan or U Street rents. That demographic shapes the neighborhood's overall relationship with cannabis: casual and normalized, increasingly treated the way residents might treat picking up a bottle of wine on the way home — a quick, routine stop rather than a special occasion.

Legal Access & Medical Cannabis Registration

Legal access in DC runs through the District's medical cannabis program, regulated by the Alcoholic Beverage and Cannabis Administration (ABCA). Columbia Heights residents who are 21 or older can self-certify online in about ten minutes without needing a doctor's visit, and many walk into District Cure with a temporary digital registration the same day they apply. Out-of-state medical cardholders can use reciprocity, and visitors without a home-state card can register temporarily on-site. Once registered, purchasing at any ABCA-licensed retailer, including District Cure, is straightforward and private.

Cannabis Use in Washington DC: By the Numbers

Washington, DC consistently ranks among the highest cannabis-use jurisdictions in the country, with survey data showing roughly a third of DC adults report using cannabis for medical or recreational purposes, a rate second only to Vermont nationally. As of spring 2026, ABCA reported more than 68 licensed medical cannabis retailers operating across the District and over 116,000 active registered patients.
